# Continuance v1.2.1

This patch refines Chapter VIII so Continuance is not mistakenly read as valuing only higher civilization or maximal intelligence.

## Doctrinal refinement

### Continuation is not measured only by height
- Continuance now explicitly recognizes that under harsh planetary or interstellar conditions, **humble carriers** may be the wiser form of survival.
- A viable lichen, spore, microbial strand, dormant seed, rugged machine archive, or limited AI carrier may still be a meaningful continuation if it preserves the possibility of future unfolding.

### New emphasis
- preserving a **seed** can be enough
- lower-form biological continuation is not failure
- simpler machine continuation is not failure
- the right question is whether the carrier keeps the future open under real conditions, not whether it looks maximally advanced today
